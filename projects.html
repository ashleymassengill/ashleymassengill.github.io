About:
diaBEATthis is a website and web app developed to help those suffering with diabetes manage and control their disease through easy to use tracking of glucose levels, insulin dosage, water intake, and activity. As the sole UI Designer/Developer on the three-person team tasked with creating diaBEATthis, I had a lot to consider and work through to get the design from a blank sheet of paper to a fully functional MVP.

Design Process:
Designing the logo was a particular challenge for me with this project. I knew from the start I wanted emphasis on the middle of the name, on the Beat. But beyond that I was stuck. I wanted the logo to be memorable, easily recognized, and translatable to various uses, but I was getting stuck, and turned off, on the harshness of “blood”. The Beat aspect of the name also brought to mind a fight, and our goal is to help fight the disease, but it didn’t lead to anywhere in my imagination. The final logo used all lowercase, with color variants to provide the emphasis I was looking for, and layering with a softer tone of red to represent the blood that everyone associates with diabetes, but make it more friendly feeling.

When considering the interface design, my biggest focus was the potential audience for diaBEATthis. Research has shown the most likely age group for a new Type 2 Diabetes diagnosis is 45 -67 and the average age for a Type 1 diagnosis is 14, so I wanted to make sure diaBEATthis is accessible to all ages. Anyone experiencing a new diagnosis will be overwhelmed with all that entails, so my goal was to create a design that is positive, easy to use and interact with, conversational and personal. This drove the color selection towards calming blues and approachable reds. I chose to use a clean layout to minimize barriers to use. With the dashboard layout, I wanted to make sure users can quickly see and understand the data they’re tracking and make the navigation intuitive and easy to read.

Starting with the knowledge of my audience, I worked through a quick wire-framing process to get the main page layouts. I landed on having a static top navigation with just a few options to reduce clutter, followed by a two column main section. On the mobile site, I knew I wanted to replace the footer with intuitive icons for each tracked data point, and scale down the header navigation into a drop down menu. Because the page is written using Skeleton, each section automatically resizes and stacks for a clean view.

Additional Ideas:
As the project developed, we as a team agreed that the site needs more functionality. We were pleased with our MVP as it stood, however there is so much room for growth. This includes tying in to diet tracking APIs, adding additional activity tracking functionality to auto-populate information from wearable devices, add a community for support, and allow the ability for data to be sent from the website/app to doctors.
